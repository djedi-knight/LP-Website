- BIOGRAPHY -

Bringing together a seamless flow of rock, rap and techo, Linkin Park is a band that is truly paving their own path in music. The roots of Linkin Park emerged in 1996 when Mike Shinoda (emcee), Brad Delson (guitarist), Joseph Hahn (DJ),Rob Bourdon (drummer), "Pheonix" Farrel (bassist) and Mark Wakefield (vocalist) started their first band, Xero.

Mike, Brad and Rob all met while attending the same high school in Southern California. Joseph was later added to the group when he and Mike met at an art school in Pasedena. The band released a four track demo tape including: Fuse, Stick N' Move, Rhinestone,and Reading My Eyes. Mark Wakefield left the band and the band changed its name to Hybrid Theory. The empty space left by Mark was soon filled by the final member of the group, Chester Bennington.

The band got their first gig at the famous L.A. club "The Whiskey", and soon after signed up with the Warner Bros. record label, and changed their name to Linkin Park. LP's debut album "Hybrid Theory" was released in October 2000. The album soon placed the band at the ear of the world, becoming multi-platinum as well as the best-selling album of 2001, selling 14 million copies worldwide to date.
